# CS-255
# Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design? What did you do particularly well?
The DriverPass project involved designing a web-based system for a client named DriverPass, a company that wanted to help students better prepare for their driving exams through online practice tests and behind-the-wheel lesson scheduling. The system included features such as customer account management, progress tracking, reporting tools, and role-based access for staff and administrators. I did particularly well translating the client’s needs into a complete system design by creating clear UML diagrams and organized requirements documentation. I also focused on building a solution that was practical, scalable, user-friendly, and aligned with the client’s overall goals.

# If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I could revise one part of this project, I would improve the UML diagrams by making the layouts cleaner and more polished for presentation purposes. While the diagrams accurately represented the system requirements and relationships, I would spend more time refining spacing, alignment, and connector placement to make them easier to read and more visually professional. I would also add a few more advanced UML details, such as clearer relationship labels and additional annotations, to strengthen the overall quality of the system design documentation.

# How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I interpreted the user’s needs by reviewing the DriverPass interview transcript and identifying the main problems the client wanted to solve, such as helping students prepare for driving exams, simplifying lesson scheduling, and improving overall business operations. I then translated those needs into system features like online practice tests, account management, progress tracking, reservation scheduling, reporting tools, and role-based access for different users. Considering the user’s needs is important because a system is only successful if it is useful, efficient, and easy for people to use. Designing with the user in mind helps ensure the final product solves real problems, improves the customer experience, and supports the goals of the business.

# How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
I approach software design by first understanding the client’s goals, users, and main problems before thinking about the technical solution. I try to break the system down into clear requirements, identify the main users and use cases, and then model the system with diagrams such as use case, activity, sequence, and class diagrams. In the future, I would continue using interview notes, requirement lists, UML diagrams, and user feedback to analyze and design systems. I would also focus on building designs that are scalable, easy to maintain, secure, and aligned with what the users actually need.
